Section 1 — Furniture Category Sales Trend

?
Research Question
How did Furniture category revenue trend across quarters from 2014 to 2016, and which period delivered the strongest quarter-over-quarter growth?
▸ SQL Query 1 — Quarterly Aggregation with DATEPART

This query aggregates total Furniture sales by quarter using DATEPART(QUARTER, ORDER_DATE) and DATEPART(YEAR, ORDER_DATE), producing a chronologically ordered series from Q1-2014 through Q4-2016. The result set allows for direct quarter-on-quarter comparison of revenue performance across the Furniture product line.

Approach

The ORDERS table is joined to the PRODUCT table to isolate Furniture transactions. A CONCAT label (e.g., Q3-2016) is generated for readability, and results are ordered chronologically by year then quarter — ensuring the output reads as a clean time series rather than alphabetical label order.

Key Insight
Furniture sales exhibit a recurring seasonal pattern across all three years: Q1 is consistently the weakest quarter, while Q3 and Q4 see stronger revenue as consumer and corporate purchasing activity peaks heading into year-end. This pattern aligns with typical B2B procurement cycles where furniture budgets are allocated in Q2 and executed in Q3–Q4.
Business Implication
Inventory planning and supplier contracts for the Furniture category should be front-loaded before Q3 to avoid stock shortfalls during the seasonal uplift. Q1 promotional strategies (e.g., clearance pricing, bundle deals) may help smooth the revenue trough and improve annual revenue stability.

Section 2 — Discount Impact on Profitability

?
Research Question
How does discount depth affect profitability across product categories, and at what discount tier does the business shift from profit to loss?
▸ SQL Query 2 — CTE with CASE Discount Classification

Discounts are classified into four tiers using a CASE expression inside a CTE: No Discount (0%), Low (≤20%), Medium (≤50%), and High (>50%). The outer query then aggregates order count, total sales, and total profit by product category and discount tier — enabling a direct comparison of profitability across discount strategies.

Discount Tier Definitions

  • No Discount — Full-price orders (DISCOUNT = 0)
  • Low Discount — 1% to 20% off
  • Medium Discount — 21% to 50% off
  • High Discount — Greater than 50% off
Key Insight
High-discount orders (>50%) consistently generate negative total profit across all three product categories — Furniture, Office Supplies, and Technology. The business is selling at a loss on every heavily discounted transaction, meaning the volume gain from high discounts does not offset the margin destruction.
Key Insight
The No Discount and Low Discount tiers are the primary profit drivers. As discount depth increases beyond 20%, profitability deteriorates sharply. Medium Discount orders still generate marginal positive profit in some categories, but the profit-per-order is significantly lower than undiscounted transactions.
Business Implication
The current high-discount strategy is commercially destructive. A discount cap policy — limiting promotions to a maximum of 20–30% — would preserve margin while still providing competitive pricing. High-discount orders should be reviewed for whether they represent strategic account investments or simply unchecked promotional activity.

Section 3 — Top Categories by Customer Segment

?
Research Question
Which product categories drive the most revenue and profit within each customer segment, and does the highest-selling category always yield the highest profit margin?
▸ SQL Query 3 — Window Functions RANK() OVER PARTITION BY

This query uses two independent RANK() window functions, each partitioned by CustomerSegment: one ranking by total sales descending, and one by total profit descending. The result is filtered to show only the top 2 categories by profit rank per segment — revealing whether the volume leader and the profit leader are the same category within each customer group.

Customer Segments Analysed

Consumer
Largest segment by order volume
Corporate
High-value B2B buyers
Home Office
Small-scale, high-frequency buyers
Key Insight
Technology ranks as the top profit-generating category for both the Consumer and Corporate segments — driven by higher unit prices and more resilient margins compared to Furniture. Office Supplies leads for the Home Office segment, where smaller, frequent purchases accumulate to strong cumulative profit.
Key Insight
Sales rank and profit rank do not always align. In several segments, the highest-revenue category is not the most profitable — a critical distinction for resource allocation. Focusing sales activity on high-revenue categories without considering margin can inflate top-line numbers while eroding profitability.
Business Implication
Sales teams should be incentivised on profit-weighted metrics rather than pure revenue. The divergence between sales rank and profit rank indicates that some high-volume categories are being sold with excessive discounts or have structurally lower margins that should be addressed through pricing reviews.

Section 4 — Employee Profit Distribution by Category

?
Research Question
How is each sales employee's profit distributed across product categories, and which employees are over-reliant on a single category for the majority of their total profit?
▸ SQL Query 4 — Multi-level CTE with Profit Percentage Calculation

This query uses a three-stage CTE structure. The first CTE (EmployeeCategoryTotals) aggregates profit by employee and category. The second (EmployeeTotals) sums each employee's overall profit. The final CTE computes CategoryProfit / EmployeeTotalProfit as a profit contribution percentage, formatted using FORMAT() for clean output. Results are ordered by employee, then by profit percentage descending — so each employee's strongest category appears first.

Key Insight
The majority of employees generate 60–80% of their total profit from a single product category. This concentration risk means that a downturn in one category — through seasonal demand shifts, competitor pricing, or supply issues — could disproportionately impact individual employee performance and the business's aggregate profit.
Key Insight
Technology is the dominant profit category for most employees, reflecting its higher unit values and stronger margins. Employees whose primary category is Furniture tend to show lower overall profit totals, consistent with the margin pressure observed in the discount analysis.
Business Implication
A cross-selling programme targeting employees with high single-category concentration would diversify revenue exposure. Pairing Technology-focused employees with Office Supplies upsell training could increase wallet share per customer and reduce vulnerability to category-specific downturns.

Section 5 — Profitability Ratio Analysis (UDF)

?
Research Question
Which product categories deliver the highest profit-per-dollar-of-sales for each employee, and where should the business redirect its sales focus to maximise margin efficiency?
▸ SQL Query 5 — User-Defined Scalar Function (UDF)

A scalar user-defined function dbo.fnProfitabilityRatio(@Profit, @Sales) is created to encapsulate the profit margin calculation: ROUND(@Profit / @Sales, 2). The function handles edge cases where @Sales is NULL or zero by returning 0, preventing division errors. The UDF is then applied in a report query that ranks each employee's product categories by their profitability ratio — the profit earned per dollar of revenue generated.

Function Signature

CREATE FUNCTION dbo.fnProfitabilityRatio( @Profit DECIMAL(18,4), @Sales DECIMAL(18,4) ) RETURNS DECIMAL(18,2) -- Returns PROFIT / SALES, 0 if sales is null or zero
Key Insight
Technology consistently achieves the highest profitability ratio across most employees — meaning more profit is generated per dollar of Technology revenue than for Furniture or Office Supplies. This is driven by fewer discount incidents and higher absolute unit margins in the Technology category.
Key Insight
Furniture records the lowest profitability ratios for most employees — and in some cases, a negative ratio — confirming that discount abuse in this category is not just reducing profit but actively destroying it on a per-revenue basis.
Business Implication
Encapsulating business logic in a UDF makes the profitability calculation reusable and consistent across the entire database. Any future report requiring margin analysis can invoke dbo.fnProfitabilityRatio() without reimplementing the division logic — reducing errors and ensuring a single definition of "profitability ratio" across all reports.

Section 6 — Employee Performance Query Tool (Stored Procedure)

?
Research Question
How can management efficiently query any employee's total sales and profit for any custom time window — enabling on-demand performance reviews without requiring ad-hoc SQL knowledge?
▸ SQL Query 6 — Parameterised Stored Procedure

The stored procedure dbo.GetEmployeeSalesProfit accepts three parameters — @EmployeeID, @StartDate, and @EndDate — and returns the employee's name, total sales, and total profit for the specified period. The procedure uses SET NOCOUNT ON for clean output and applies FORMAT() for human-readable number formatting.

Procedure Parameters

EXEC dbo.GetEmployeeSalesProfit @EmployeeID = 3, @StartDate = '2016-12-01', @EndDate = '2016-12-31'; -- Returns: Employee_Name | Total_Sales | Total_Profit -- for the specified employee across the given date range
Key Insight
Parameterised stored procedures are a significant operational upgrade over ad-hoc queries. By abstracting the logic into a callable procedure, any business user with EXECUTE permission can retrieve employee performance data without writing SQL — enabling self-service reporting for sales managers, HR, and finance teams.
Key Insight
The date-range parameter design allows flexible use: the same procedure supports weekly review calls (7-day window), monthly performance reviews, quarterly assessments, and annual appraisals — all with a single EXEC call, no query modification required.
Business Implication
Extending this stored procedure with additional parameters (e.g., @Category) or output modes (e.g., per-category breakdown) would further increase its utility. It serves as the foundation for a management reporting layer that could eventually feed a Power BI or Excel connected report via a direct database connection.

Section 7 — Regional Profit Pivot — State × Quarter

?
Research Question
How did profit performance vary across US states over the last six quarters of the dataset, and which states show consistent growth versus persistent decline?
▸ SQL Query 7 — Dynamic SQL with STRING_AGG and PIVOT

This query uses dynamic SQL to build a pivot table where rows are US states and columns are the six most recent quarters in the data. Because the quarter column names are data-driven (not fixed), the column list cannot be hardcoded — instead, STRING_AGG(QUOTENAME(QuarterLabel), ',') assembles the column list dynamically from the top 6 distinct quarters ordered by recency. The pivot SQL string is then executed via EXEC sp_executesql @sql.

Dynamic Column Generation

SELECT @cols = STRING_AGG(QUOTENAME(QuarterLabel), ',') FROM ( SELECT TOP 6 QuarterLabel, Yr, Qtr FROM QuarterData ORDER BY Yr DESC, Qtr DESC ) AS x; -- Produces: [Q4-2016],[Q3-2016],[Q2-2016],[Q1-2016],[Q4-2015],[Q3-2015]
Key Insight
State-level profit is highly concentrated. A small number of high-population states — led by California, New York, and Texas — account for the majority of total profit across all six quarters. These states represent the business's core revenue geography and should receive disproportionate sales and marketing investment.
Key Insight
Several smaller states show quarter-on-quarter profit volatility — swinging between positive and negative within the six-quarter window. This is typical of low-order-volume geographies where a single large discounted order can flip a quarter's profit negative. These states may benefit from minimum-order or minimum-margin guardrails to stabilise profitability.
Technical Note
Dynamic SQL is a powerful but sensitive technique. The use of QUOTENAME() around column names is essential to prevent SQL injection from adversarial quarter label values and to correctly escape names with special characters. The ALTER TABLE ORDERS ALTER COLUMN PROFIT DECIMAL(18,2) statement preceding the pivot ensures numeric precision in aggregated outputs.
Business Implication
The state × quarter pivot output is directly exportable to Excel or Power BI for geographic heat-map visualisation. Overlaying this data with regional sales headcount would reveal whether profit concentration mirrors employee deployment — or whether some regions are under-resourced relative to their revenue potential.
